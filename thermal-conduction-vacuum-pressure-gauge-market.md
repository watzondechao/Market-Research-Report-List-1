<p><p>The thermal conduction vacuum pressure gauge market includes two primary types: thermal conductivity type and thermal expansion type. Thermal conductivity gauges measure pressure based on changes in the thermal conductivity of a gas within a vacuum environment, making them suitable for low-pressure applications. In contrast, thermal expansion gauges rely on the expansion of a fluid or solid in response to temperature changes, providing reliable measurements at varying pressures. Both types are essential for precise vacuum measurement across diverse industries.</p></p>
