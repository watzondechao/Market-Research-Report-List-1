<p><p>Rayon Grade and Specialty Grade DWP refer to different classifications of dissolving wood pulp used in the production of rayon and other specialty applications. Alkali wood pulp or sulfate wood pulp is manufactured through a chemical process that retains cellulose while removing lignin, making it suitable for high-quality rayon production. Conversely, wood pulp of sulfite is created using a different chemical method, often resulting in a lower grade. Both types serve specific markets in textiles and specialty products, emphasizing purity and fiber quality.</p></p>
