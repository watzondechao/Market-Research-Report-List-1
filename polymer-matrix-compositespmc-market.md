<p><p>Polymer Matrix Composites (PMCs) are materials made from a polymer resin combined with reinforcing fibers, offering high strength-to-weight ratios and versatility. The market for PMCs is primarily divided into two types: thermosets and thermoplastics. Thermoset composites, which cure into a rigid structure, are known for their durability and heat resistance, often used in aerospace and automotive applications. Thermoplastic composites, in contrast, can be re-melted and reshaped, allowing for easier manufacturing and recycling, making them popular in a variety of industries including consumer goods.</p></p>
