<p><p>The automotive active axle steering market is primarily divided into two types: active front axle steering and active rear axle steering. Active front axle steering enhances vehicle maneuverability and stability by adjusting the front wheels' angles in response to speed and driving conditions. Conversely, active rear axle steering improves handling and cornering by varying the angle of the rear wheels, allowing for sharper turns at low speeds and increased stability at higher speeds. Both systems contribute to superior driving dynamics and safety.</p></p>
