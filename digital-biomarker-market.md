<p><p>The digital biomarker market is divided into two primary types: passive and active digital biomarkers. Passive digital biomarkers are automatically collected data from devices, like wearables or smartphones, that monitor health indicators without user intervention, providing continuous insights into a person's health. In contrast, active digital biomarkers require user engagement, such as prompts for surveys or tests, where individuals provide data through deliberate actions. Both types offer valuable information for health monitoring, disease management, and personalized medicine strategies.</p></p>
