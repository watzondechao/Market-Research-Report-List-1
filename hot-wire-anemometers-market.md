<p><p>Hot-wire anemometers are devices used to measure fluid velocity, utilizing a thin wire heated by an electric current. The market includes two primary types: side heating and direct heating. Side heating types involve positioning the heated wire alongside the flow, allowing for accurate velocity measurements without direct interference. In contrast, direct heating types use the wire placed directly within the flow, providing rapid response times and precise readings, making them suitable for various applications in fluid dynamics and aerodynamics.</p></p>
