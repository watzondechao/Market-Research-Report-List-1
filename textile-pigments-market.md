<p><p>The textile pigments market includes various types like Disperse, Reactive, Sulfur, VAT, Acid, and others. Disperse pigments are used for synthetic fibers, offering vibrant colors. Reactive pigments bond chemically with fibers, ensuring durability and wash-fastness, ideal for cotton. Sulfur pigments are cost-effective, used for dark shades on cellulosic fibers. VAT pigments provide excellent lightfastness, suitable for high-quality fabrics. Acid pigments are used for protein fibers like wool and silk. Other pigments cater to niche applications, offering versatility and specialized properties for diverse textile needs.</p></p>
