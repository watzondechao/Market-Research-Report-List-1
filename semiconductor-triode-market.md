<p><p>The semiconductor triode market includes two main types: NPN and PNP triodes. NPN triodes consist of a layer of p-type semiconductor sandwiched between two n-type layers, allowing current to flow from the collector to the emitter when a positive voltage is applied to the base. Conversely, PNP triodes have an n-type layer between two p-type layers, allowing current to flow when the base is at a lower voltage than the emitter. Both types are essential in amplifying and switching applications in electronics.</p></p>
